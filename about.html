A brief explanation of each continent

Asia

Asia is the largest continent by area and the world's eighth-largest by population. It encompasses most of the Earth's modern-day Asia, which includes China, India, the Southeast Asian nations, and the Malay Peninsula. Asia is the only continent with both naturally occurring and human-induced landforms.

Africa

Africa is the largest continent by area and the world's third-largest by population. It is bounded by the Atlantic Ocean on the south, the Indian Ocean on the southwest, and the North African and Southern African Oceans on the northeast and northwest, respectively. Africa is home to over 200 nations, most of which are members of the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN).

North America

North America is the largest continent in the world by area and the world's fourth-most populous. It comprises the North American continent, which includes Canada, the United States, the Bahamas, the United Kingdom, and several smaller islands, as well as various territories in the Caribbean, the Atlantic Ocean, and the Pacific Ocean.

South America

South America is the largest continent in the world by area and the world's fifth-most populous. It comprises the South American continent, which includes Brazil, Argentina, Chile, Colombia, Venezuela, Peru, Ecuador, Bolivia, Guyana, Suriname, French Guiana, Paraguay, and the Americas. South America is the only continent with both naturally occurring and human-induced landforms.

Europe

Europe is the third-largest continent by area and the world's most populous. It comprises the continent of Europe, which includes nations such as Germany, France, the Netherlands, Belgium, the United Kingdom, Italy, Spain, Portugal, and Switzerland. Europe is the only continent with both naturally occurring and human-induced landforms.

Australia

Australia is the largest continent in the world by area and the world's second-most populous. It is bounded by the Tasman Sea on the southwest, the Southern Ocean on the south, and the Indian and Pacific Oceans on the northeast and northwest, respectively. Australia is the world's most populous country, with over 25 million people.

Antarctica

Antarctica is the smallest continent in the world by area and the world's fifth-smallest by population. It is bounded by the Southern Ocean on the south, and the Arctic Ocean on the north. Antarctica is the only continent with naturally occurring landforms, and it is home to the world's largest ice sheet, the Antarctic Ice Sheet.
